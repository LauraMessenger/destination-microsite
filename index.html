Colorado, Rocky Mountains National Park is the perfect place for adventurers, and outdoor people to spend a weekend or longer. The Park offers many trails to hike that you will never find yourself bored or asking what now. Spend your days hiking Water Fall, Summit, and Lake Trails.

Lake Hike

We have provided a list of the most popular types of trails and their difficulties

Bear Lake Nature Trail

We recommend for the first-time hikers or families with really young children Bear Lake Nature Trail. This trail has a difficulty ranking of easy.

Cublake Trail Loop

More experienced hikers and families with older children we recommend Cublake Trail Loop. This lake hike has a difficulty ranking of medium.

Mills Lake, Black Lake, Frozen Lake, and Blue Bird Lake

For the experienced professional hikers, we recommend Mills Lake, Black Lake, Frozen Lake, and Blue Bird Lake. These trails have a difficulty ranking of hard.

Water Fall Hike

Alberta Falls Trail, Adams Falls Trail, and Cascade Falls

We recommend for the first-time hikers or families with really young children Alberta Falls Trail, Adams Falls Trail, and Cascade Falls. These trails have a difficulty ranking of easy.

Quzel Falls

More experienced hikers and families with older children we recommend Quzel falls. This water fall hike has a difficulty ranking of medium.

Timberline Falls

For the experienced professional hikers, we recommend Timberline Falls. This trail has a difficulty ranking of hard.

Submit Hike

Deer Mountain Trail

More experienced hikers and families with older children we recommend Deer Mountain Trail. This summit hike has a difficulty ranking of medium.

Twin Sisters Peak Trail and Flat Top Mountain Trail

For the experienced professional hikers, we recommend Twin Sister Peak Trail and Flat Top Mountain Trail. These trails have a difficulty ranking of hard.
